The Ultimate Checklist: How to Prepare Your AC for the Summer Season

📅 Published: March 31, 2026 👤 Author: Andrew Sandman ⏱ 10 min read

Every year, when the first heatwave hits your location, HVAC companies get flooded with thousands of emergency calls. Most of these breakdowns could have been avoided with 30 minutes of spring preparation. Modern air conditioners are robust, but they are not "set and forget" machines. Dust, debris, and dormant electrical components need attention before they are pushed to their limits in 35°C heat.

Step 1: Inspect the Outdoor Condenser Unit

During winter, the outdoor unit becomes a magnet for dried leaves, twigs, and dust. If you used a cover for the winter, remove it before turning the AC on. Check the aluminum fins: if they are clogged with "poplar fluff" (typical for your location in June) or dirt, the unit won't be able to dissipate heat, leading to compressor overheating.

Step 2: Clean the Indoor Filters

This is the most important step you can do yourself. Open the front panel of your indoor unit and slide out the mesh filters. If they are covered in a grey layer of dust, your AC's efficiency drops by 15-20%. Wash them with plain lukewarm water, let them dry completely, and slide them back in. Pro tip: Never use a hair dryer to dry them; the heat can warp the plastic mesh.

Step 3: Check the Condensate Drain Line

Stagnant water in the drain line over winter can lead to mold or even small nests from insects. If the line is blocked, water will back up and leak inside your room, damaging your wallpaper or electronics. Pour a small cup of water into the internal drain pan to ensure it flows freely to the outside.

💡 Why Spring Maintenance Saves Money

A clean system uses up to 30% less electricity. In 2026, with rising energy costs, an unmaintained AC can cost you an extra $50-$100 over the summer in wasted power alone. Moreover, regular cleaning prevents the "Dirty Sock Syndrome"—that musty smell caused by bacteria build-up on the evaporator coils.

Step 4: Test the Thermostat and Controls

Change the batteries in your remote control. Turn the AC on in "Cool" mode (not just "Fan") and set it to a temperature 5 degrees lower than the current room temp. Listen for unusual clicking or grinding sounds. Within 5-10 minutes, you should feel a steady stream of cold air.

When Should You Call a Professional?

While DIY cleaning is great, some things require specialized tools and certification. You should book a professional AC-Master visit if:

  • The air isn't cold despite clean filters (indicates low refrigerant).
  • There is a vibration in the outdoor unit (fan motor issues).
  • You haven't had a "Deep Cleaning" with antibacterial foam in over 2 years.
  • The electrical connections show signs of wear or burning.

Conclusion

Taking the time to prepare your air conditioner in April or May ensures a stress-free summer. You'll breathe cleaner air, pay lower bills, and avoid the "peak season" waiting lists for repair techs.
