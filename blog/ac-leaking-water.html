Why is My AC Leaking Water Inside the House? (And How to Stop It)

📅 Published: March 31, 2026 👤 Author: Andrew Sandman ⏱ 9 min read

Finding a puddle of water on your floor or a damp spot on your wallpaper right under the air conditioner is a nightmare for any homeowner. While a cooling system naturally produces moisture (condensate) as it dehumidifies your room, that water is supposed to stay inside the unit and drain outdoors. If it's leaking inside, something is wrong.

1. A Clogged Condensate Drain Line

This is the #1 cause of AC leaks. As the unit pulls moisture from the air, it also pulls in dust, pet dander, and mold spores. Over time, these particles combine with water to form a sludge or "jelly" that blocks the narrow PVC drain pipe. When the water has nowhere to go, it overflows the internal drain pan and pours down your wall.

2. Dirty Air Filters

You might wonder: "How can a dry filter cause a water leak?" It's simple. A dirty filter blocks airflow over the evaporator coil. When the coil doesn't get enough warm air to blow over it, it gets too cold and freezes over. When that ice eventually melts, it produces more water than the drain pan can handle, leading to a major leak.

3. A Cracked or Rusted Drain Pan

If your air conditioner is more than 10-12 years old, the internal drain pan (usually made of plastic or metal) might have developed a crack or rusted through. In this case, water simply falls through the hole before it ever reaches the drain line. This requires a professional replacement of the pan or the entire indoor casing.

🛑 Immediate Action: What to do now?

  1. Turn off the AC: Stop the cooling process to prevent more condensate from forming.
  2. Mop up the water: Prevent damage to your flooring and electronics below the unit.
  3. Check the filters: If they are filthy, you've found your culprit.

4. Broken Condensate Pump

In some installations (especially in offices or basements), the water cannot drain by gravity and is pushed out by a small electric pump. If the pump loses power or the motor fails, the water will backup immediately. You can usually tell if you have a pump if there is a small white box attached to the side or bottom of your unit.

5. Improper Installation (Leveling Issues)

If your AC was recently installed and it's already leaking, it might not be level. The indoor unit must be perfectly horizontal or tilted slightly toward the drain hole. Even a 2-degree tilt in the wrong direction can cause water to pool at the opposite end of the pan and overflow.
