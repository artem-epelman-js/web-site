How to Prepare Your AC for the Summer Heat

As temperatures begin to rise, preparing your AC isn't just about comfort; it's about preventing a mid-July breakdown when technicians are busiest...

First, replace your air filters. A clogged filter restricts airflow and forces the motor to work overtime. Second, clear the area around your outdoor condenser unit. Remove dried leaves, weeds, or debris that might block heat dissipation. Third, test your thermostat by switching it to cooling mode early in the season.

5 Signs Your Air Conditioner Needs a Freon Refill

Refrigerant (Freon) is the lifeblood of your AC. If it's low, you likely have a leak. Learn how to spot the signs before your unit stops cooling entirely.

  • Air isn't cold: The most obvious sign of low refrigerant.
  • Ice on coils: Low levels cause the evaporator coil to freeze.
  • Hissing noises: Often indicates a gas leak in the copper lines.
  • High bills: The AC runs longer to reach target temperatures.

Top Benefits of Regular AC Maintenance

Skipping annual maintenance might seem like a way to save money, but it often leads to much higher costs down the road.

Regular professional check-ups provide three major advantages: extended equipment lifespan (fixing small issues before they destroy the compressor), improved air quality (preventing mold spores), and significant energy savings by keeping the unit at its peak SEER rating.

Why Your AC is Leaking Water: Common Causes

Finding a puddle under your indoor unit can be alarming. The most common culprit is a clogged condensate drain line.

Over time, algae and dust form a sludge that blocks the pipe, forcing water to back up. Other causes include a damaged drain pan in older units or a dirty evaporator coil that causes the unit to freeze and then drip excessive amounts of water as it melts.
